To you, and you alone, what matters is the process: the experience of shaping that artwork. The viewers’ concerns are not your concerns (although it’s dangerously easy to adopt their attitudes.)More often, though, fears rise in those entirely appropriate (and frequently recurring) moments when vision races ahead of execution.vision is always ahead of execution, knowledge of materials is your contact with reality, and uncertainty is a virtue.The development of an imagined piece into an actual piece is a progression of decreasing possibilities, as each step in execution reduces future options by converting one — and only one — possibility into a reality.Art happens between you and something — a subject, an idea, a technique — and both you and that something need to be free to move.
